## Bottom Line

The stock/near-stock visual Pong lane should have worked, and it did work once
the checkpoint horizon was long enough. The earlier flat reads for `s114`,
`s120`, and `s121` were premature. The current failure lesson is not “these
seeds cannot learn”; it is “do not judge sparse Atari Pong from tiny horizons
or score-only evals.”

The custom/debug lanes should not be counted as failed Pong replications. They
were useful for plumbing, wrappers, speed, or external controls, but their flat
curves do not say LightZero cannot learn Pong.

Next check: keep maturing the stock Pong rows, raise the eval cap for strong
checkpoints, and carry the same survival-first measurement discipline into any
CurvyTron training claim.
